Inspirations and areas of study 09/13/2013

Julian Casablanca, 11th dimention

Excellent performer and entertainer. Music is highly atmospheric and video highly theatrical. Performance art to the highest degree. Focus on techniques of theatre especially regarding blending music, imagery, and possibly the most important aspect the lyrics. It has been my conclusion through studying his performance that the lyrics are a key aspect of creating the atmosphere. Timed correctly body language, the musical harmonies, a high attention and awareness of costume as well as set, and spoken word. It appears if one has the mind to bring all of these aspects together for certain moments of the song in a way that conveys the artists interpretation, expectations, emotions, and projections the atmosphere can be altered to a high degree. It’s not surprising that with the use of four or more variables working in sync with one another can produce such a profound change in one's perception. Studying this branch of thought further will surely be beneficial in that learning the art of performance, especially musical performance, is a vital and integral part of any musicians abilities.

The Gorillaz

Superb yet simplistic (although can be complex at times, a few songs in odd times, very large and intelligent focus on not only producing solid music but entertainment to a degree not touched by most groups.) From their cartoon personalities to their tracks written with simply telling a story. Arguably the most common form of communication up until the advent of modern communication the ability to orally communicate a sequence of events and consequences was often the key role and only method of learning important things. The art of storytelling, whether it be sung, spoken, or drawn is an invaluable method of communicating to your audience. The Gorillaz seem to be the epitome of this.

Tech 9

What more can I say they are simply amazing, lyrically, rhythmically, musically, hard to find a better at what they do. I've spent many a night studying, recreating, and trying to learn as much as I can from this group.

"People perceive you to be lethal when you ain't see through like tissue."

With lyrics like these it's no wonder they've been such a success. From studying tech I've learned that killer lyrics are a vital part to any excellent song with a well build atmosphere.

Well thought out, intelligent, out of the box, amazing lyrics. It’s in this area that my work is focused, without lyrics people care about you just have some nice sounding noise. To gain that extra dimension that’s such an integral part of any successful musicians abilities they must master the art of the language.

Paul Simon

This guy knew how music was supposed to be made, a true musician if I've ever heard one. If there was ever someone to study the music of this is the guy. A quote from an interview regarding his songwriting method "The music always precedes the words. The words often come from the sound of the music and eventually evolve into coherent thoughts. Or incoherent thoughts. Rhythm plays a crucial part in the lyric-making as well. It’s like a puzzle to find the right words to express what the music is saying." While my method of songwriting doesn't always follow this formula I often find that when not actively planning and constructing a song it often naturally finds it's way through these steps. After a few setbacks involving the extended planning of a song that was, well, much too extended, I have leaned more toward Pauls method. So far it's a time saving and idea forming miracle. It allows the ideas to come to me while I'm working on the other parts of the song that are more obvious. I feel as if I've cut my songwriting time almost in half after taking this advice to heart.

Foo Fighters

Have always enjoyed the art from this group but didn't fully appreciate it until I recently discovered a song of theirs called Learn to Fly. It blew me away, there are not a lot of pop songs being created by pop artists in ODD TIMINGS. I'm not saying 4/4 is something that shouldn't be done or is necessarily overdone but pop artists could broaden their range of time signatures quite a bit more than they are. I believe creating a song in a long time (15/8) and have it be odd creates a sort of swing, if done correctly, which is almost intoxicating in its listening pleasurability. If you're having trouble understanding what I mean or simply are unaware of what time signatures are just listen to a few of these songs I've gathered that are in odd times (in addition to the foo fighters link above.) See if you can figure out the time signature on your own, or if you don't know how listen carefully and see if you can hear the difference between these and other pop songs in common 4/4 time (choose any pop song and it's likely to be 4/4).

Solsbury Hill

This song... I can hardly put into words how much I enjoy this song. It has atmosphere, subtle yet amazing aspects of theatre, and most importantly a song that I would argue has few equals.
